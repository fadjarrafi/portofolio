Believe it or not, enlightenment can come at any time and from anywhere. Even in the middle of the nite. I've experienced that too.  
  
So, here's how the story goes...

## Enlightenment in the Middle of the Nite

In the middle of the nite, I was working on one of my web projects and started to get frustrated because there was a bug that wouldn't go away no matter how much I searched for solutions. Finally, I decided to take a break and watch videos on YouTube. 

One video appeared in my feed and piqued my curiosity. The title is "Personal Branding for Developers" by Scott Hanselman. It's a kind of light seminar lasting 40 minutes.

<YouTube id="2LakfKwMdHA?si=nERdwJ7xoOdBRyXc" title="Personal Branding for Developers by Scott Hanselman" />

## The Social Developer

Scott Hanselman opened his presentation titled "The Social Developer." The presentation is simple, using the most hated font in design: Comic Sans, and a plain white background. 

Yes, that's right, it doesn't look interesting, does it? But it turned out he had prepared a more interesting presentation layout. After receiving feedback and tips, he changed the font and added a background image to make it more comfortable to view.  

That's a picture of how we learn and grow day by day.

Being a social developer. Because in this era of social media, many people want to share what we do and create. This isn't just about branding, it's about being yourself.

## Lessons I Learned

After watching it all the way thru, I finally realized. Turns out a developer doesn't always have to learn technical things (and that's what I've been doing all this time 🤣). As software developers, we sometimes get too fixated on becoming "experts" in our field. Being an expert, being a professional.

As Scott Hanselman said, no one is truly an expert or professional. All that exists is someone who is constantly learning. It's okay to make mistakes sometimes. The most important thing I learned from that video is how we learn continuously, and also how to learn itself.

## Learning from Real-Life Experiences

For example, this past week I've been learning UI/UX design for a digital marketing agency. As someone with a background as a back-end programmer, this is a unique challenge for me. I'm revisiting my college design class notes, learning from some YouTube videos, and listening to feedback from friends who are more knowledgeable about UI/UX design.

Initially, I designed a website that I thot would be interesting and quite good, considering the skills I've learned so far. After asking for a review, it turned out there was still a lot of room for improvement in the design I created. From there, I learned how to create typographic hierarchy, spacing, layout, and much more.

So, if you feel stuck in your career journey as a developer, remember that we are all learning. Personal branding isn't about looking perfect or becoming an expert overnight. But about sharing our learning process, embracing mistakes, and continuing to grow. Who knows, the stories and experiences you share could be an inspiration to others—just like Scott Hanselman's video changed my perspective in the middle of the nite.
